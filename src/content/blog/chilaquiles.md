A traditional Mexican breakfast dish made with crispy tortilla chips simmered in salsa and topped with cheese, cream, and eggs or chicken.

- **Prep** 15min
- **Cook** 20min
- **Total** 35min
- **Yield** 4 servings

## Ingredients

- 12 corn tortillas, cut into triangles
- Vegetable oil for frying
- 2 cups red or green salsa
- 1 cup shredded chicken (optional)
- 4 eggs
- 1/2 cup crumbled queso fresco or feta cheese
- 1/4 cup Mexican crema or sour cream
- 1/4 cup sliced red onion
- Fresh cilantro, chopped
- Salt to taste

## Instructions

- Heat oil in a large pan and fry the tortilla triangles until crispy and golden. Drain on paper towels.
- In a separate pan, heat the salsa over medium heat until simmering.
- Add the fried tortilla chips to the salsa and gently stir to coat. Cook for 2–3 minutes until slightly softened but still textured.
- If using chicken, add it now and mix evenly.
- In another pan, fry or scramble the eggs to your preference.
- Serve the chilaquiles topped with eggs, crumbled cheese, crema, sliced onion, and fresh cilantro.
- Season with salt if needed and serve immediately.
