A classic Latin American dish made with ripe or green plantains, fried until golden and crispy or caramelized. Served as a snack, side dish, or dessert.

- **Prep** 10min
- **Cook** 10min
- **Total** 20min
- **Yield** 4 servings

## Ingredients

- 3 ripe plantains (for sweet version) or green plantains (for savory version)
- Vegetable oil (for frying)
- Salt to taste (for savory version)
- Optional: sugar, honey, or condensed milk (for sweet version)

## Instructions

- Peel the plantains and cut them into slices. For sweet plantains, cut diagonally into thick pieces; for savory, slice into thin rounds or strips.
- Heat a generous amount of oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat until hot.
- Add the plantain pieces in batches, avoiding overcrowding.
- Fry for 3–5 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden brown and cooked through.
- Remove and place on paper towels to drain excess oil.
- Season with salt (savory) or drizzle with sugar, honey, or condensed milk (sweet).
- Serve immediately while hot and crispy.
